Feeling... lonely? You are not alone

64% of college students said they had felt “very lonely” from a 2017. We believe that virtal learning has further increased these feelings of loneliness, and have compiled some strategies for you.

How to Manage Feelings of Loneliness During COVID

Symptoms of loneliness:

  • Gained Weight
  • Getting sick often
  • Being surrounded by negative people [more likely to feel lonely if you are surrounded by negative people]
  • Being significantly influenced by the people they keep close
  • Constantly checking social media [studies show that using social media sites increase levels of misery + loneliness, also we become addicted to social media when we’re feeling a lack of social interaction in real life]

Try these tips

  • Keep to a Schedule
  • Stay Informed (while also limiting media consumption to an extent)
  • Stay Active
  • Do Something Meaningful
  • Connect with Others
  • Find Sources of Comfort
  • Create Something
  • Find Healthy Distractions (watching tv, listening to music, read)
  • Practice self compassion
  • Make plans for the future

Key Takeaways

Focus on your friendships and relationships with loved ones. Get comfortable in your own company [go to the movies alone, keep a gratitude journal, etc.]. Reach out to others and practice small talk (that is friendly + inviting). Pursue a hobby.

“Young people often feel quite isolated and alone,” agrees clinical psychologist Lisa Firestone, PhD, director of research and education for the Santa Barbara, California-based Glendon Association. “They often feel excluded, and I think the pandemic just exacerbates these feelings of being an outsider.”
